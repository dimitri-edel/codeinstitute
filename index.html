Biography

Bruce was born in San-Fransisco, CA, USA in 1940. He grew up in British Hong-Kong and started actiong at a very young age. Bruce also took up martial arts and dancing classes as an adolescent. At the age of 18, being an American citizen, he moved from Hong-Kong to the US. He enrolled at the University of Washington To make money he started a martial arts school in Seatle, WA.

Bruce Lee dreamed of becoming a famous actor in Hollywood. It took him long enough but he got there eventually. Bruce also made a name for himself in the world of martial arts. His impact on the world of martial arts was huge. Because of his aproach to hand-to-hand combat a whole new breed of martial art sprang up some 20 years after his death. Bruce introduced the idea of Mixed Martial Arts and layed the foundation for the sport. Nowadays, many clubs exist that allow fighters from varoous backgrounds to compete with one another. The first and most famous of these clubs is probably the UFC.

Philosophy

Bruce studied Taoism since he was a teenager. Taoism at its core is about maintaining balance.

His insights and inferences are universal. We all know that if you swerve too far into the negative poeple will reject you. And if you swerve too far into the positive people will take advantage of you. Even in Mathematics, the Game Theory states that the best way to go is to open up with a cooperative move and then mirror the actions of your opponent. Which one could translate to being kind and respectful to people when you meet them. If they react with the same kindness and repect you just do the same. Yet if they start beeing disrespectful then one should act accordingly. I am not suggesting that one should stoop to their level. On the contrary, there are many ways to remain polite and still comand respect. The only thing one should not do is keep pampering someone even though they are being clearly inconsiderate and rude. Bruce once said:I am not in this world to live up to your expectations and you are not in this world to live up to mine. In predicaments of that nature you need to correct the trajectory of the interaction towards something positive. Try to find common ground and build on that. This is the ideo of beeing centered. It requires a certain level of self-awareness. Staying calm but ready to spring into action if necessary.

Another angle of his philosophy is to strive for flexibility. For there is one certainty in life that things will change. And if you fail to react to the changes in your environment you will be left behind. For instance, in competition is it necessary to keep close tabs on one's rivals. And if they change their tactics or strategy, so should you if you do not yet have a counterstrategy. Undeniably all relevant changes in the world require us to change. And I believe this is one way of regarding his famous quote Be like Water!.

Another angle of his philosophy is to learn from anyone and anything if you consider it useful. Build on that knowledge and get creative with it. But also reject anything that does not agree with you. And again this requires a high level of self awareness.
